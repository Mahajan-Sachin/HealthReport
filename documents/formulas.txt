--- eGFR (CKD-EPI 2021 Race-Free Creatinine Equation) ---

Formula:
  eGFR = 142 × min(Scr/κ, 1)^α × max(Scr/κ, 1)^(-1.200) × 0.9938^Age × Sex_factor

Where:
  Scr = Serum Creatinine in mg/dL
  κ (kappa) = 0.7 for females, 0.9 for males
  α (alpha) = -0.241 for females, -0.302 for males
  Sex_factor = 1.012 for females, 1.0 for males
  Age = age in years

Units: mL/min/1.73m²

Interpretation:
  >= 90  → Normal (Stage 1 if kidney damage present)
  60-89  → Mildly reduced (Stage 2)
  30-59  → Moderately reduced (Stage 3)
  15-29  → Severely reduced (Stage 4)
  < 15   → Kidney failure (Stage 5)

--- LDL Cholesterol (Friedewald Equation) ---

Formula:
  LDL = Total_Cholesterol - HDL - (Triglycerides / 5)

Units: mg/dL
Valid only when Triglycerides < 400 mg/dL
If TG >= 400 mg/dL: use direct LDL measurement

--- VLDL Cholesterol ---

Formula:
  VLDL = Triglycerides / 5

Units: mg/dL
Normal range: 5 - 40 mg/dL

--- Non-HDL Cholesterol ---

Formula:
  Non-HDL = Total_Cholesterol - HDL

Units: mg/dL
Optimal: < 130 mg/dL
Includes all atherogenic lipoproteins (LDL + VLDL + IDL)

--- BMI (Body Mass Index) ---

Formula:
  BMI = Weight_kg / (Height_m)²

Interpretation (WHO):
  < 18.5   = Underweight
  18.5-24.9 = Normal weight
  25.0-29.9 = Overweight
  30.0-34.9 = Obesity Class I
  35.0-39.9 = Obesity Class II
  >= 40.0  = Obesity Class III (Severe)

Asian-specific cutoffs (India/WHO):
  < 18.5   = Underweight
  18.5-22.9 = Normal
  23.0-24.9 = Overweight
  >= 25.0  = Obese

--- Anion Gap ---

Formula:
  Anion Gap = Na - (Cl + HCO3)

Normal: 8 - 12 mEq/L
HIGH = metabolic acidosis (DKA, lactic acidosis, poisoning)

--- Albumin/Globulin Ratio (A/G Ratio) ---

Formula:
  A/G Ratio = Albumin / Globulin
  Globulin = Total Protein - Albumin

Normal: 1.1 - 2.5
LOW A/G = liver disease, chronic inflammation, autoimmune

--- BUN/Creatinine Ratio ---

Formula:
  BUN/Cr Ratio = BUN / Creatinine

Normal: 10:1 to 20:1
HIGH (> 20): dehydration, high protein diet, GI bleeding
LOW (< 10): liver disease, malnutrition, muscle wasting

--- Transferrin Saturation ---

Formula:
  Transferrin Saturation = (Serum Iron / TIBC) × 100

Normal: 20 - 50 %
LOW = iron deficiency
HIGH = iron overload (hemochromatosis)

--- Coronary Risk Ratios ---

Total Cholesterol / HDL Ratio:
  Optimal: < 4.0
  Normal: 4.0 - 5.0
  Elevated risk: > 5.0

LDL / HDL Ratio:
  Optimal: < 2.5
  Borderline: 2.5 - 3.5
  High risk: > 3.5

--- Unit Conversions ---

Glucose:
  mg/dL → mmol/L: divide by 18.0182
  mmol/L → mg/dL: multiply by 18.0182

Cholesterol/Lipids:
  mg/dL → mmol/L: divide by 38.67
  mmol/L → mg/dL: multiply by 38.67

Creatinine:
  mg/dL → µmol/L: multiply by 88.42
  µmol/L → mg/dL: divide by 88.42

HbA1c:
  % → mmol/mol: (% - 2.15) × 10.929
  mmol/mol → %: (mmol/mol / 10.929) + 2.15

Vitamin D:
  ng/mL → nmol/L: multiply by 2.496
  nmol/L → ng/mL: divide by 2.496
