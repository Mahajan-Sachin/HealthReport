--- COMPLETE BLOOD COUNT (CBC) ---

Hemoglobin (Hb):
  Men: 13.5 - 17.5 g/dL
  Women: 12.0 - 15.5 g/dL
  Children (6-12 yrs): 11.5 - 15.5 g/dL
  Pregnant: 11.0 - 14.0 g/dL
  LOW = Anemia. HIGH = Polycythemia or dehydration.

Red Blood Cell Count (RBC):
  Men: 4.5 - 5.9 million/µL
  Women: 4.0 - 5.2 million/µL

Hematocrit (HCT/PCV):
  Men: 41 - 53 %
  Women: 36 - 46 %

Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV):
  Normal: 80 - 100 fL
  LOW = Iron deficiency or thalassemia. HIGH = B12/folate deficiency.

Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH):
  Normal: 27 - 33 pg

Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration (MCHC):
  Normal: 32 - 36 g/dL

Red Cell Distribution Width (RDW):
  Normal: 11.5 - 14.5 %

White Blood Cell Count (WBC/TLC):
  Normal: 4,000 - 11,000 /µL (or 4.0 - 11.0 x10^9/L)
  LOW = Immunosuppression, viral infections. HIGH = Infection, inflammation.

Neutrophils (Absolute/Differential):
  Normal: 50 - 70 % (or 1,800 - 7,700 /µL absolute)

Lymphocytes:
  Normal: 20 - 40 % (or 1,000 - 4,800 /µL absolute)

Monocytes:
  Normal: 2 - 8 %

Eosinophils:
  Normal: 1 - 4 % (HIGH = allergy, parasitic infection)

Basophils:
  Normal: 0 - 1 %

Platelet Count (PLT):
  Normal: 150,000 - 400,000 /µL
  LOW (<150,000) = Thrombocytopenia. HIGH (>400,000) = Thrombocytosis.

--- LIPID PROFILE ---

Total Cholesterol:
  Desirable: < 200 mg/dL
  Borderline High: 200 - 239 mg/dL
  High: >= 240 mg/dL
  mmol/L equivalent: < 5.2 / 5.2-6.2 / >= 6.2

LDL Cholesterol (Low-Density Lipoprotein — "bad"):
  Optimal: < 100 mg/dL
  Near Optimal: 100 - 129 mg/dL
  Borderline High: 130 - 159 mg/dL
  High: 160 - 189 mg/dL
  Very High: >= 190 mg/dL

HDL Cholesterol (High-Density Lipoprotein — "good"):
  Men: > 40 mg/dL (optimal > 60 mg/dL)
  Women: > 50 mg/dL (optimal > 60 mg/dL)
  LOW HDL = increased heart disease risk

Triglycerides:
  Normal: < 150 mg/dL
  Borderline High: 150 - 199 mg/dL
  High: 200 - 499 mg/dL
  Very High: >= 500 mg/dL

VLDL Cholesterol:
  Normal: 5 - 40 mg/dL

Non-HDL Cholesterol:
  Optimal: < 130 mg/dL
  Calculated as: Total Cholesterol - HDL

--- BLOOD GLUCOSE / DIABETES ---

Fasting Blood Glucose (FBG/FBS):
  Normal: 70 - 99 mg/dL
  Prediabetes: 100 - 125 mg/dL
  Diabetes: >= 126 mg/dL (on two separate occasions)
  mmol/L: Normal 3.9-5.5 / Prediabetes 5.6-6.9 / Diabetes >=7.0

Postprandial / Post-meal Glucose (2hr PPG):
  Normal: < 140 mg/dL
  Prediabetes: 140 - 199 mg/dL
  Diabetes: >= 200 mg/dL

Random Blood Glucose:
  Normal: < 140 mg/dL
  Diabetes (with symptoms): >= 200 mg/dL

HbA1c (Glycated Hemoglobin — 3-month average):
  Normal: < 5.7 %
  Prediabetes: 5.7 - 6.4 %
  Diabetes: >= 6.5 %
  Well-controlled diabetes target: < 7.0 %

Insulin (Fasting):
  Normal: 2 - 25 µIU/mL

--- KIDNEY FUNCTION TEST (KFT / RFT) ---

Serum Creatinine:
  Men: 0.74 - 1.35 mg/dL
  Women: 0.59 - 1.04 mg/dL
  HIGH = kidney may not be filtering well

Blood Urea Nitrogen (BUN):
  Normal: 7 - 20 mg/dL
  HIGH = kidneys not clearing waste efficiently

BUN/Creatinine Ratio:
  Normal: 10:1 to 20:1

Uric Acid:
  Men: 3.4 - 7.0 mg/dL
  Women: 2.4 - 6.0 mg/dL
  HIGH = gout risk, kidney stones

eGFR (Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate — CALCULATED):
  >= 90 mL/min/1.73m² = Normal kidney function (Stage 1 if with kidney damage)
  60 - 89 = Mildly reduced (Stage 2)
  30 - 59 = Moderately reduced (Stage 3 CKD)
  15 - 29 = Severely reduced (Stage 4 CKD)
  < 15 = Kidney failure (Stage 5 CKD)

Sodium (Na):
  Normal: 136 - 145 mmol/L
  LOW (hyponatremia): dehydration, heart/kidney issues

Potassium (K):
  Normal: 3.5 - 5.1 mmol/L
  HIGH (hyperkalemia): kidney disease, dangerous for heart

Chloride (Cl):
  Normal: 98 - 107 mmol/L

Bicarbonate (HCO3):
  Normal: 22 - 29 mmol/L

Calcium (Ca):
  Normal: 8.5 - 10.5 mg/dL (or 2.1 - 2.6 mmol/L)

Phosphorus:
  Normal: 2.5 - 4.5 mg/dL

--- LIVER FUNCTION TEST (LFT) ---

SGPT / ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase):
  Men: 7 - 56 U/L
  Women: 7 - 45 U/L
  HIGH = liver cell damage (hepatitis, fatty liver, alcohol)

SGOT / AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase):
  Normal: 10 - 40 U/L
  HIGH = liver or heart muscle damage

Alkaline Phosphatase (ALP):
  Normal: 44 - 147 U/L
  HIGH = bile duct issues, bone disease

Gamma-GT / GGT:
  Men: 8 - 61 U/L
  Women: 5 - 36 U/L
  HIGH = alcohol use, liver/bile issues

Total Bilirubin:
  Normal: 0.1 - 1.2 mg/dL
  HIGH = jaundice risk (liver/bile problems)

Direct Bilirubin:
  Normal: 0.0 - 0.3 mg/dL

Indirect Bilirubin:
  Normal: 0.2 - 0.9 mg/dL

Total Protein:
  Normal: 6.0 - 8.3 g/dL

Albumin:
  Normal: 3.4 - 5.4 g/dL
  LOW = liver disease, malnutrition, kidney loss

Globulin:
  Normal: 2.0 - 3.5 g/dL

A/G Ratio (Albumin/Globulin):
  Normal: 1.1 - 2.5

Free T4 (Thyroxine):
